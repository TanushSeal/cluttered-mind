My interview experience at the Indian Association for the Cultivation of Science(IACS), Kolkata

Date: July 1, 2025 Place: Kolkata

Post Code: BLG2025-001

I was shortlisted for appearing the interview for the Integreted PhD program in Mathematics at IACS, Kolkata. I appeared for the interview on June 27, 2025. The panel consisted of Professors, Dr. Alok Goswami and Dr. Mithun Mukherjee.

The interview:

AG: Please Take a seat.

Me: Thank You, Sir.

AG: Could You please tell us where you have done your bachelors from?

Me: Institute of Mathematics and Application, Bhubaneswar

AG: What topic are you comfortable with?

Me: Real Analysis

AG: Just Analysis...

He was looking for something more so I said metric spaces.

AG: Do you know, when are two metrics said to be equivalent?

Me: If they induce the same topology.

I gave this version of the definition because, I had told them I had studied metric spaces as part of Topology.

AG: Do you know of some condition which is sufficient to say that two metrics are equivalent?

Me: If both of them are discrete metrics.

This answer isn't technically wrong, but I might as well have said that If the metrics are exactly same. LOL! I guess, they were looking for this result:

Theorem:&⁡Let, d1 and d2 be two metrics on a set X. If there exists two real numbers k1 and k2> 0 such that

k1d1(x, y) ≤ d2(x, y) ≤ k2d1(x, y) for all x,y ∈ X, then the metrics d1 and d2 are equivalent.

AG: No, but, I will give you a problem based on what you are thinking, go to the board.
